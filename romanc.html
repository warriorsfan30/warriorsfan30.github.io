Roman Christianity

Section 1

The Romans were very religious people. They held festivals to honor the gods. The Romans were also very practical, and they used it in their religious lives. They weren’t sure which gods were real, so they worshipped all the gods. The Roman allowed people they conquered to keep their beliefs. Sometimes, the romans even took their beliefs and used them in their religion. For example, when the Romans invaded greece, they used their gods in roman religion.They also used gods from the Egyptians, Gauls, and Persians. Roman leaders though Judaism was a potential problem. The Jews didn’t believe in many gods, and thought their god was the only god. Some romans thought they were insulting them by not worshiping their gods. Political conflict was starting between the Jews and the Romans. In 63 BC, the Jews rebelled, as they wanted to be in a land ruled by a Jew. They were defeated and punished for their actions. Another revolt happened in the 100’s and Emperor Hadrian, tired of stopping jewish revolts, he banned the practice of some Jewish rituals. He thought it would make people to give up judaism and their desire of independence. That just made one more revolt, and the Roman Army crushed the revolt, destroyed the Jewish capital of Jerusalem, and forced all Jews to leave that city. The Romans built a new city on the ruins of Jerusalem, and brought settlers to live there. The Romans banned the Jews from visiting the city more than once a year.
